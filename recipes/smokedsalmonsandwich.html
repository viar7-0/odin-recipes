Smoked Salmon Sandwich

This smoked salmon sandwich makes for a fresh, flavorful and healthy meal, easy to pack for a picnic lunch or prep as a light weeknight dinner. Made with sliced smoked salmon, an herby and bright cream cheese and crème fraiche spread kissed with dill, chives and lemon, plus thinly sliced red onion, cucumber and micro greens, this smoked salmon sandwich is a delicious little cafe-inspired offering!

Ingredients:

  • 6 ounce demi-baguette (sliced in half vertically to make two pieces, then sliced in half) horizontally and lightly toasted
  • 1 large Persian cucumber, sliced thinly (about 1.5cup worth)
  • Micro arugula or regular arugula greens (about 1 cup)
  • cream cheese
  • a quater cup thinly sliced red onion
  • 6 to 8 ounces sliced smoked salmon
  • Sprigs of fresh dill (optional topping)

Preparation:

  1. Start by spreading the cream cheese over the bread you choose. You can toast the bread for an extra kick of texture if you prefer.
  2. Begin piling gradually the toppings. Start with the greens as they are light in weight and can take the heavier toppings.
  3. Add in smaller toppings like radishes, cucumbers and sliced onions
  4. Now you’re going in with the smoked salmon. Layer as much or as little as you like.
  5. he smoked salmon is quite salty, so we skipped the salt. We opted for black pepper and chilli flakes.
  6. To finish the smoked salmon sandwich, we put the dill. It ’s a DREAM in this sandwich!
